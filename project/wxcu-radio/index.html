About WXCU

WXCU Radio is a student-run internet radio station at Capital University in Columbus, Ohio. WXCU Radio broadcasts exclusively online through its website, streaming 24/7. My time at WXCU was transformational, spanning roles from on-air DJ to completely rebranding the station, coding their new WordPress website, organizing major philanthropic events, and winning community awards.

On-air master control room at WXCU Radio.

Starting as a DJ

I first took to the airwaves on my own show in mid-September 2013. With training on the RCS Master Control software, I started developing what songs my show would play and what personality my show would have. Shortly after, I joined up with future regular collaborator, Maura, to do a weekly themed specialty show. Each week, we chose a theme, and picked music that fit with the theme.

Rebranding & Design

When I first came to WXCU, it had no consistent brand message, no consistent idea, and the website was in dire straights. Working with the student Management Team, I created a new logo and brand identity for WXCU. The only direction I got as far as creating a new logo was “Modern.” Since "modern" seems to mostly just mean simple, bold, and thin, I started in that direction.

With some criticism and a suggestion to include the school colors purple and white, we eventually settled on a refined, bold version set in Myriad Pro Black. The WXCU purple and black color variants became standard across the station.

Web Development

Having completed a web development class at Capital, I asked the station team if I could completely rebuild and modernize their website. After teaching myself how to integrate custom HTML, CSS, and PHP into WordPress, we launched a modern, media-friendly streaming portal at the end of January 2014.

For the fall 2015 semester, I completely revamped the website with a modern responsive grid, prominent live-stream player controls, dynamic show scheduling, and a clean backend structure for DJs to publish content easily.

Events & Radiothon

In the fall of 2014, we began drafting plans for a charity event. Radio and charity have collaborated for years, so we organized Capital University's first-ever Radiothon for the spring of 2015—a 24-hour non-stop broadcasting event designed to raise funds for the Furniture Bank of Central Ohio.

As part of the 24-hour event, we filled the airwaves with creative specialty shows, live local band performances, and on-air challenges that raised significant support and established WXCU as a philanthropic force on campus.

Awards & Recognition

Our hard work culminated in multiple major recognitions:

  • In Spring 2015, WXCU Radio won Capital University's Organization of the Year award.
  • In December 2015, the station received an official nomination from the Intercollegiate Broadcasting System (IBS) for best Streaming/Online only Radio Station in the nation.
  • In April 2016, Capital University recognized Radiothon 2016 as the Community Engagement Program of The Year.
