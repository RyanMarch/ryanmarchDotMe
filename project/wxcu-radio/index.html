About WXCU

WXCU Radio is a student-run internet radio station at Capital University in Columbus, Ohio. WXCU Radio broadcasts exclusively online through its website, streaming 24/7. My time at WXCU was transformational, spanning roles from on-air DJ to completely rebranding the station, coding their new WordPress website, organizing major philanthropic events, and winning community awards.

Starting as a DJ

I first took to the airwaves on my own show in mid-September 2013. With training on the RCS Master Control software, I started developing what songs my show would play and what personality my show would have. Shortly after, I joined up with future regular collaborator, Maura, to do a weekly themed specialty show. Each week, we chose a theme and picked music that fit with the theme.

Rebranding & Design

When I first came to WXCU, it had no consistent brand message, no consistent idea, and the website was in dire straights. Working with the student Management Team, I created a new logo and brand identity for WXCU. The only direction I got as far as creating a new logo was “Modern.” Since "modern" seems to mostly just mean simple and bold, I started in that direction.

With some criticism and a suggestion to include the school colors purple and white, I created a few more ideas. We eventually settled on the top-left version. The letters are set in Myriad Pro Black. I created a few color variations.

First round of logo concepts exploring various geometric styles.
Refining the chosen layout and preparing final variations, with university colors.

With some revisions and a decision to include the school colors purple and white, we eventually settled on a refined, bold version set in Myriad Pro Black. The WXCU purple and black color variants became standard in most uses going forward. I created a few other color variations for different uses.

Color variations for different uses.

At the time, WXCU suffered from a lack of awareness around the school. And the people that did know what WXCU was, didn't think much about it. I wanted to present WXCU as a professional organization, and make sure it was clear who we are and what we do, while still expressing a sense of humor. To promote WXCU around campus, I created a series of "punny" typographic posters.

The real fun came when we ordered stickers to give away at our events. Stickers started showing up around campus: on boards, notebooks, and laptops. We also ordered T-shirts - because what respectable radio station doesn't have T-shirts? These efforts helped make WXCU immediately recognizable around campus.

To complete the branding, the radio station is always formally introduced as WXCU Radio. This helps the public immediately grasp what we do. We synchronized this handle across all platforms, securing consistent branding with the URL wxcuradio.com, and the username wxcuradio on Facebook, Twitter, and Instagram, and other online services.

Web Development

Now we needed a website. I had just completed a web development class at Capital and asked the team if I could rework the website. After much struggling, I taught myself how to work with HTML, CSS, and PHP through WordPress. We finally launched the new site and the new logo at the end of January 2014:

The old, outdated wxcuradio.com site circa October 2013.
Rebuilt wxcuradio.com stream launch in January 2014.

In the next semester, I completely revamped the website, keeping mind the lessons learned from the previous iteration. I updated the design with a modern responsive grid, prominent live-stream player controls, dynamic show scheduling, and a clean backend structure for DJs to publish content easily.

Upgraded, responsive WordPress site, deployed in 2015.

Events & Radiothon

In the fall of 2014, we started creating plans for a charity event. We organized Capital University's first-ever Radiothon for the spring of 2015. We created a 24-hour non-stop broadcasting event designed to raise funds for the Furniture Bank of Central Ohio.

Envisioned as a 24-hour event, we had to figure out how to fill all of that time with compelling content both on and off the air. We partnered up with the Furniture Bank of Central Ohio on the recommendation from one of our staff. After months of prep work and nervous nights, we launched the 24-hour live broadcast, raising significant support and establishing WXCU as a philanthropic force on campus.

As part of the 24-hour event, we filled the airwaves with creative specialty shows, live local band performances, and on-air challenges that raised significant support and established WXCU as a philanthropic force on campus.

Building on that success, we organized Radiothon 2016 the following spring, further solidifying the event as a hallmark annual campus tradition. The 2016 event tripled our fundraising efforts from the previous year.

Awards & Recognition

Our hard work culminated in multiple major recognitions:

  • In Spring 2015, WXCU Radio won Capital University's Organization of the Year award.
  • In December 2015, the station received an official nomination from the Intercollegiate Broadcasting System (IBS) for best Streaming/Online only Radio Station in the nation.
  • In April 2016, Capital University recognized Radiothon 2016 as the Community Engagement Program of The Year.
